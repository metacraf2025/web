Glass Partitioning

Glass partitioning systems come in various types like framed, frameless, and sliding, each offering distinct balances of privacy, light transmission, and functionality for offices, homes, and commercial spaces. Common materials include tempered, laminated, or acoustic glass with customizable finishes.

Frame System
Frame Glass Partitioning

Framed glass partitions use slim aluminum or steel frames around tempered glass panels to provide structural support and a polished, professional look in offices and commercial spaces. They balance durability with aesthetics, offering more stability than frameless options while allowing light transmission.

  • Aluminum frames (20-50mm wide) in anodized, powder-coated, or wood-grain finishes for corrosion resistance and customization; supports single or double glazing up to 12mm thick.
  • Modular panels (typically 1200mm wide x floor-to-ceiling height) with swing, sliding, or fixed doors, including acoustic seals and hardware like handles or locks.
  • Options for frosting, tinting, or branding on glass for privacy; demountable design for easy reconfiguration without wall damage.
Frame System
Semi Glass Frame Partitioning

Semi-framed glass partitions combine minimal framing—typically at the top and bottom channels—with large glass panels on the sides for a balanced, modern look that offers more support than fully frameless systems. They provide structural stability while maintaining high transparency and light flow in offices and commercial interiors.

  • Slim aluminum profiles (15-50mm wide at top/bottom) accommodating 8-12mm toughened glass; side edges often edge-to-edge for semi-frameless effect.
  • Modular assembly with channels like GP2510 (41.5mm wide x 25mm thick) or GP18100 (18x100mm) supporting single/double glazing and VIG options.
  • Includes sliding/swing doors, acoustic seals, and powder-coated finishes; heights up to 3m with easy joinery via brackets and clips.
Frameless System
Frameless Glass Frame Partitioning

Frameless glass partitions employ single large sheets of toughened or laminated glass secured by minimal top/bottom tracks or patch fittings, eliminating visible frames for a seamless, ultra-modern appearance. They maximize transparency and light flow in offices, homes, and retail spaces while providing privacy options via frosting or tinting.

  • Glass thickness from 8-16mm (tempered, low-iron, or LCD privacy glass) with panel widths up to 60" and heights to 12ft floor-to-ceiling; supports curved or faceted designs.
  • Slim aluminum tracks (1"x1" or 1"x4") in anodized or custom RAL colors, with pivot, sliding, or hinged doors; STC ratings up to 47 for acoustics.
  • Hardware includes patch fittings, bottom guides, and integral blinds for easy installation and relocatability without wall alterations.
